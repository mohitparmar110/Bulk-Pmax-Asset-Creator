⚡ Features

Everything your creative ops team needs.

OverlayForge is purpose-built for performance marketers managing multiple brands across Google and Meta.

🏢

Per-brand organisation

Every brand gets its own folder. Upload images, overlays, and export independently. Switch between brands in one click.

🎯

6 ad formats, one export

Covers all Google PMax (1200×1200, 1200×628, 960×1200) and Meta (1920×1080, 1080×1920, 1080×1080) sizes automatically.

Exact-pixel matching

No stretching. No cropping. Overlays only apply when the image is the exact right size. Mismatches go into an unmatched folder.

Organised ZIP output

Download a ZIP with images sorted into folders by size. Drop straight into your campaign manager or share with your media buyer.

💾

Persistent storage

Your brands, images, and overlay settings are saved in your browser. Come back tomorrow and everything's exactly where you left it.

📋 Process

From upload to export in under 60 seconds.

No training required. If you can drag and drop, you can use OverlayForge.

1

Create your brand folders

Add a brand with a name and colour. Repeat for every client or product you manage. No limits.

❓ FAQ

Common questions.

Are my images ever uploaded to a server? +
No. OverlayForge runs entirely in your browser using the HTML5 Canvas API and IndexedDB. Your images are processed locally and never sent anywhere. This makes it safe for confidential client assets.
What image formats are supported for upload? +
You can upload JPG, PNG, and WebP images as your base images. Overlays must be PNG (to support transparency). You can export results as either PNG or JPG.
What if my image doesn't match any overlay size? +
Images that don't exactly match one of the 6 supported sizes are placed into an "unmatched" folder inside the ZIP. They're exported as-is so you don't lose any files.
Do I need to upload all 6 overlays for a brand? +
No. You can upload only the sizes you need. Images whose size has no matching overlay are placed in the unmatched folder. You can work with just 1 size if that's all you need.
Do my brands and images persist when I close the browser? +
Yes. Everything is saved using IndexedDB in your browser — your brands, uploaded images, and overlay files are all retained across sessions. Clearing your browser data will remove them.
